About GPA

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is a numerical representation of a student's academic performance, calculated by averaging the grades received in all of their courses. Typically, each course is assigned a certain number of credits, and the GPA is weighted by these credits.

GPA scales vary depending on the educational institution, but commonly range from 0.0 to 5.0. In some systems, an A grade (typically representing excellent performance) is equivalent to a 4.0, while an F grade (representing failure) is equivalent to 0.0.

GPA is an important indicator of a student's academic achievement and is often used by educational institutions for admission purposes, scholarship eligibility, and academic standing.
